A brief timeline of Aleister Crowley's life.

  • 1875 - Born to a pair of deeply religious evangelical Christians in Warwickshire, England.
  • 1887 - Crowley's father, Edward, dies of cancer. This is the point of no return for Crowley, as he becomes completely hostile to Christianity and revels in the titles bestowed upon him by his own mother: "The Great Beast", "the unholy monster of the Apocalypse (Therion, 666)".
  • 1896 - While on holiday in Sweden, Crowley had a "majoy mystical experience" originating from his first same-sex sexual encounter. This would be the start of his highly promiscuous life while enjoying all genders.
  • 1898 - Crowley is initiated into the Hermetic Order of the Golden Dawn by the group's leader, Samuel Liddell MacGregor Mathers. He adopts the magical name of "Frater Perdurabo".
  • 1904 - On a trip to Cairo, Egypt, Crowley claimed to have heard the voice of "Aiwass", his Holy Guardian Angel. During sessions of automatic writing this holy entity dictated the text of the 'Book of the Law'— the central sacred text that would form the basis of Thelema.
  • 1907 - Leaves the Order of the Golden Dawn and in collaboration with fellow occultist George Cecil Jones creates the A∴A∴, the Argenteum Astrum, through which they begin to build and propagate the religion of Thelema
  • 1912 - Initiated into a German-based esoteric order, the Ordo Templi Orientis (O.T.O.), and rose to become the leader of its British branch. He reformulated the branch in accordance with his Thelemite beliefs.
  • 1920 - Founded the Abbey of Thelema in Cefalù, Sicily where he lived with his various followers. Their libertine lifestyle led to denunciations in the British press.
  • 1923 - Evicted from Sicily by the Italian government following the mysterious death of a young Englishman residing at the abbey.
  • 1947 - Died in Hastings, England at the age of 72. Crowley's last words were reportedly, "I am perplexed". The last doctor to have treated him died within 24 hours of Crowley's death— supposedly of a curse the magician had put on him.
